Let's Adjust Some Features

A common way router companies compete is by adding features. Many times these features can cause potential security problems and generally, the less features you have running, the safer your network becomes.

Below is a list of features you can switch off, and we've set it up for you by default. If you decide you need it on, that is fine. But if you don't, leave it off.

Features

A visitor to your home shows why WPS is a danger. The visitor only needs to turn your router upside down, take a picture of the label and they can, thereafter, get into your Wi-Fi network. The WPS password on the router label is essentially, a get out of jail free card in Monopoly. It over-rides the WPA2 password.
Universal Plug and Play (UPnP) allows network devices to automatically forward a port on your router. Unless you are a heavy user of applications that need port forwarding, like peer-to-peer applications, you are safer leaving this disabled.
>Disabling this will cause your SSID to not appear when a device is searching for a network. You can still connect by manually typing in the name.
